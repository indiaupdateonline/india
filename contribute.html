Contribute to the Archive

Help preserve and share India's literary heritage through translations, research, and community support

Join Our Mission

The Indian Literature Archive thrives through the contributions of scholars, translators, and enthusiasts like you

The Indian Literature Archive is a collective effort to preserve and make accessible the vast literary heritage of India. We rely on contributions from scholars, translators, researchers, and volunteers to grow our collection and improve our resources.

Whether you're an academic, a language expert, a student, or simply passionate about Indian literature, there are many ways you can contribute to this important cultural preservation effort.

Ways to Contribute

Choose how you'd like to participate in this important work

Contribute Translations

We're always seeking skilled translators to help make classical and medieval Indian texts accessible to wider audiences. Whether you're translating from Sanskrit, Tamil, or any other Indian language into English or between Indian languages, your work can help preserve these important texts.

We accept both new translations and previously published translations (with proper permissions). All translations undergo peer review to ensure quality and accuracy.

Requirements for Translators:

  • Native or near-native proficiency in both source and target languages
  • Background in literary studies or relevant field preferred
  • Ability to provide contextual notes and commentary
  • Willingness to work with our editorial guidelines
Submit a Translation

Contribute Research

Scholars and researchers can contribute critical editions, commentaries, annotations, or original research articles related to Indian literature. Your expertise can help contextualize these works for modern readers and academic audiences.

We welcome submissions on textual analysis, historical context, literary criticism, comparative studies, and other scholarly approaches to Indian literature.

Opportunities for Researchers:

  • Write critical introductions to texts in our collection
  • Contribute scholarly articles to our journal
  • Participate in peer review of submissions
  • Help identify gaps in our collection
Submit Research

Volunteer Your Skills

Even if you're not a translator or scholar, you can still contribute valuable skills to our project. We need help with proofreading, web development, community outreach, social media, and many other areas.

Volunteers are the backbone of our organization, helping us keep costs low while maintaining high standards of quality and accessibility.

Volunteer Opportunities:

  • Proofreading and editing translations
  • Digitizing and transcribing texts
  • Web development and design
  • Social media and community outreach
  • Event planning and coordination
Become a Volunteer

Support Financially

As a non-profit initiative, we rely on donations to fund our operations, pay translators and researchers, and maintain our digital infrastructure. Your financial support helps ensure these literary treasures remain accessible to all.

Donations of any size make a difference. We also welcome institutional sponsorships and grants to support specific projects or language collections.

Ways to Give:

  • One-time or recurring donations
  • Sponsor a specific text or translation project
  • Corporate matching programs
  • Planned giving and bequests

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about contributing to the archive

We require contributors to license their work under a Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International License. This means:

  • You retain copyright of your work
  • Others must give you credit when using your work
  • Your work cannot be used for commercial purposes without permission
  • You are free to publish your work elsewhere as well

For special cases, we can discuss alternative licensing arrangements.

As a non-profit initiative with limited funding:

  • Most contributions are voluntary
  • We offer honorariums for certain priority projects when funding is available
  • Academic contributors may be able to use their work for professional credit
  • We provide letters of acknowledgment and can serve as a reference
  • For larger projects, we sometimes seek grant funding that includes compensation

If compensation is important for your participation, please indicate this in your submission and we'll discuss possibilities.

All scholarly contributions undergo peer review:

  1. Initial screening by our editorial team
  2. Review by 2-3 experts in the relevant field/language
  3. Double-blind process (reviewers don't know author's identity and vice versa)
  4. Reviewers evaluate accuracy, scholarship, and clarity
  5. Editor compiles feedback and works with author on revisions
  6. Final approval by editorial board

The process typically takes 4-8 weeks depending on the length and complexity of the submission.

  • We prefer works that are out of print or difficult to access

Financial contributions support:

  • Translator honorariums: 45% of funds go to compensating translators for priority projects
  • Technology: 25% for maintaining and improving our digital platform
  • Outreach: 15% for educational programs and community engagement
  • Administrative: 10% for essential operational costs
  • Preservation: 5% for physical conservation of rare texts

We publish annual financial reports detailing all income and expenditures.

Volunteer commitments vary by role:

  • Proofreaders/Editors: Typically 2-5 hours per week
  • Transcribers: Flexible, often project-based
  • Web Developers: 5-10 hours monthly for maintenance
  • Social Media: 1-3 hours weekly
  • Event Volunteers: Occasional as needed

We ask for a minimum 3-month commitment for most roles to ensure continuity. Volunteers can adjust their availability as needed and take breaks when necessary.
